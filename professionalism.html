Professionalism in Instruction

Professionalism is the bedrock of trust between an instructor and a pupil — especially when you’re being assessed.

More Than Just Driving

Being a professional ADI is not only about technical driving. It’s your consistency, preparation, boundaries, and how you represent the profession in public. On a Standards Check, professionalism often shows up in the small moments: calm tone, clear structure, and safe decision-making under pressure.

Professional Appearance

Your appearance sets the tone. Aim for smart, practical, and comfortable clothing suitable for driving. Avoid overly casual wear — you want pupils (and parents) to feel confident from the first minute.

Coaching prompt: “If a parent watched the first 3 minutes of your lesson, what impression would they get?”

Essential Documents Checklist

Always ensure the following are valid and available during lessons and the Standards Check:

  • Valid Driving Licence (Original)
  • ADI/PDI Badge (Correctly Displayed)
  • Insurance with Instruction Cover
  • MOT & Service Records (for car safety)
  • Pupil Progress Records (Logbooks)

Common Professional Slips

  • Turning up rushed: no lesson structure, no goal agreement.
  • Over-talking: giving instructions instead of coaching decisions.
  • Blurry boundaries: “mates” vibe that reduces authority and safety.
  • Ignoring reflection: not debriefing errors into learning points.
Examiner lens: Are you demonstrating a professional, safe, and structured approach — even when the pupil makes a mistake?
