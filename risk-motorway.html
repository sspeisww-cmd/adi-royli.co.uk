Hazard Perception

Why Hazard Perception Matters?
Proactive hazard perception is the foundation of defensive driving. It requires scanning far ahead and anticipating potential dangers before they occur, giving the driver time to react calmly and safely.

Common Learner Errors

  • Fixated Vision: Focusing only on the car directly ahead instead of scanning the wider environment.
  • Delayed Reaction: Failing to recognize hazards early, leading to heavy braking instead of smooth slowing.
  • Overconfidence: Not maintaining a safe following distance, reducing reaction time significantly.
